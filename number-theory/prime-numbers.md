# Prime Numbers – Basics

## Definition
A prime number is a natural number greater than 1
that has exactly two positive divisors: 1 and itself.

## Examples
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 are prime numbers.

## Basic Properties
- Every integer greater than 1 can be written as a product of primes.
- There are infinitely many prime numbers.

## Small Observation
All primes greater than 3 are of the form 6k ± 1.

## Olympiad Relevance
Prime numbers appear frequently in number theory
and divisibility problems.
